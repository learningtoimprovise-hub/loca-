Verified Client
Bloom Organics

A Real Story

How Riya's launch went from
invisible to unforgettable.

I.
The Problem

Riya Malhotra co-founded Bloom Organics out of her Lucknow apartment in early 2024 — a small-batch skincare line built on cold-pressed ingredients and zero greenwashing. The product was exceptional. The reach wasn't. Three months post-launch, her best reel had 800 views. She'd tried two influencer platforms before, both left her with fake-follower reports and a bruised budget.

II.
Finding LOCA

A mutual contact in Gorakhpur mentioned LOCA. Riya reached out expecting a pitch deck. Instead, she got a 45-minute discovery call where the team asked about her customer's Saturday morning routine — not her follower target. "They were trying to understand my buyer, not sell me a package," she recalls. Within a week, they had shortlisted seven micro-creators in the wellness-meets-minimalism space — all with audiences under 80K, all with engagement above 6%.

III.
The Campaign

LOCA built a "Morning Ritual" content arc — creators showed their real skincare routines with Bloom products woven in naturally. No forced unboxings, no discount codes shouted at the camera. The briefs were specific enough to protect the brand voice, loose enough to let creators be themselves. Content went live across three weeks in October 2024.

I was terrified it would look like every other sponsored post. It didn't. People were DMing the creators asking if it was paid. That's when I knew LOCA had done something real.

Riya Malhotra
Co-Founder · Bloom Organics, Lucknow
